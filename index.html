01 · About

A brief overview.

I hold a PhD in Telematic Engineering from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (Cum Laude, International Mention, 2022), earned at IMDEA Networks Institute under the supervision of Prof. Vincenzo Mancuso and Prof. Marco Ajmone Marsan. Since May 2022 I have been a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Murcia, within the Intelligent Systems and Telematics group led by Prof. Antonio Skarmeta.

My research focuses on how we can make next-generation mobile networks — particularly 6G — secure by design, trustworthy in operation, and resilient under stress. This involves policy-based security management, end-to-end trust frameworks across distributed cloud-to-edge environments, and the integration of AI/ML for network automation.

Before joining Murcia, I spent six months as a visiting scholar at Paderborn University (Germany, 2019), working with Prof. Falko Dressler on opportunistic communication paradigms. Earlier in my career I combined academia with industry, working as Testing Analyst and Project Manager at Telefónica de España (2014–2016) before starting my doctoral studies at IMDEA Networks.

02 · Research

What I work on.

/01

6G Security & Trustworthiness

End-to-end security architectures, trust models and resilience frameworks for the 6G end-to-end system, with transfer of research outcomes to 3GPP standardization.

/02

Distributed Cloud-to-Edge Architectures

Security and management controls for distributed virtualized networks, service orchestration, and network slicing across cloud-native environments.

/03

AI/ML for Network Automation

Federated and serverless distributed learning, large language models applied to industrial communications, and AI-driven network management (AIMLF).

/04

Opportunistic & Mobile Communications

Device-to-device networking, opportunistic content dissemination, storage capacity and mobility-aware protocols — a research line rooted in my doctoral studies.
